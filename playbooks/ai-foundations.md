# 🚀 AI Foundations for Startups: From Vision to Proof of Concept with Azure AI

### 1-Day Whiteboarding Workshop for Software Development Startups

---

## 🧭 Workshop Overview

This 1-day session helps **software development startups** understand how to adopt and scale AI using Microsoft’s **Cloud Adoption Framework for AI (Startup Path)** — focusing on creating **actionable outcomes** such as AI Proofs of Concept (PoCs) and MVPs that deliver business value.

Based on the AI Adoption Framework that you can find [here](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/azure/cloud-adoption-framework/scenarios/ai/), this workshop combines strategic alignment, technical education, and hands-on planning to help startups rapidly move from AI curiosity to AI capability.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

By the end of this workshop, each participant will:

- Understand the **AI Foundations journey** within the Microsoft Cloud Adoption Framework (CAF) – Startup Path.
- Identify **specific, high-impact AI use cases** within their product or business.
- Map their use cases to the right **Azure AI services** (AI Foundry, Agents, Copilot, Search, etc.).
- Define a **30-day PoC plan** with business-aligned success metrics.
- Learn the essentials of **Responsible AI and governance** for startups.

---

## 👥 Audience

- Founders and CTOs of SaaS or ISV startups  
- Product Managers and Technical Leads  
- AI Engineers, Developers, and Architects

## 🧠 CAF Startup Path – Key Stages

| CAF Stage | Focus | Example Activities |
|------------|--------|--------------------|
| **Understand** | Assess opportunity and data readiness. | Identify business pain points and available datasets. |
| **Envision** | Design a strategic AI use case. | Define MVP, metrics, and success criteria. |
| **Build** | Develop and test an AI PoC or MVP. | Prototype using Azure AI Foundry + Copilot + Search. |
| **Scale** | Integrate and operationalize AI capabilities. | Move PoC into SaaS production stack. |
| **Govern** | Apply responsible AI, security, and cost control. | Implement policy guardrails and monitoring. |
